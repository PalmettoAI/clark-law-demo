## Cold-Call Talking Points
1. **Solo/small firm, no marketing staff** — Gail is likely doing everything herself. She has zero time to manage a website or think about SEO.
2. **Estate planning is a trust-based sale** — the website must convey credibility instantly. A beautiful, modern site does that in 3 seconds.
3. **Her ideal client is a Lexington family with aging parents or a new home** — they Google "estate planning attorney Lexington SC" and whoever shows up first wins.
4. **She's competing with McCutchen McLean, Dooley, Salley** — all general practice. She's the estate planning specialist. That's a differentiation story worth telling on the web.
5. **Real estate closings = volume work** — a site with an online intake form could pre-qualify clients and save her hours per week.

## Pain Points to Address
- No meta description on her current site = invisible to Google
- No schema markup = Google doesn't know she's a lawyer
- "By appointment" office hours suggest she's time-constrained — automation (contact forms, intake) is a real value-add
- Probate clients are often distressed families — a warm, trustworthy site converts them

## Key Differentiators to Highlight
- 20+ years in Lexington specifically (local trust factor)

- Downtown Lexington address (established, accessible)
